Kadhi Recipe

Description

Kadhi or karhi is a yogurt-based dish originating from the Indian subcontinent. It is made by simmering yogurt with besan (gram flour) and Indian spices until it forms a thick, tangy gravy. Sometimes, it is also mixed with pakoras (Source: Wikipedia).

Ingredients

  • For Kadhi:
    • 1 cup Yogurt (curd)
    • 3 tbsp Besan (gram flour)
    • 3 cups Water
    • 1 tsp Turmeric Powder
    • 1 tsp Red Chili Powder
    • 1 tsp Coriander Powder
    • 1 tsp Salt (adjust to taste)
  • For Pakoras:
    • 1 cup Besan (gram flour)
    • ½ cup Water (adjust as needed)
    • 1 small Onion (chopped)
    • 1 Green Chili (chopped)
    • ½ tsp Ajwain (carom seeds)
    • ½ tsp Red Chili Powder
    • ½ tsp Baking Soda (optional, for fluffiness)
    • Salt to taste
    • Oil for frying
  • For Tempering (Tadka):
    • 2 tbsp Oil or Ghee
    • 1 tsp Mustard Seeds
    • 1 tsp Cumin Seeds
    • 1 Dry Red Chili
    • 1 sprig Curry Leaves
    • 1 tsp Ginger-Garlic Paste
    • ½ tsp Asafoetida (Hing)
  • For Garnishing:
    • 2 tbsp Chopped Coriander Leaves

Steps

  1. Prepare Kadhi Mixture
    • In a bowl, whisk 1 cup yogurt with 3 tbsp besan until smooth.
    • Add 3 cups water and mix well.
    • Stir in 1 tsp turmeric powder, 1 tsp red chili powder, 1 tsp coriander powder, and salt.
    • Keep aside.
  2. Make Pakoras
    • In a bowl, mix 1 cup besan, ½ cup water, chopped onion, green chili, ajwain, red chili powder, baking soda, and salt.
    • Whisk until the batter is smooth and slightly thick.
    • Heat oil in a pan and drop spoonfuls of batter into hot oil.
    • Fry until golden brown and crispy. Remove and drain on paper towels.
  3. Cook Kadhi
    • Heat 1 tbsp oil in a pan.
    • Add 1 tsp mustard seeds, 1 tsp cumin seeds, 1 dry red chili, and curry leaves.
    • Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a few seconds.
    • Pour the prepared kadhi mixture into the pan.
    • Stir continuously on medium heat until it starts boiling.
    • Simmer for 15-20 minutes until it thickens.
  4. Add Pakoras & Final Tempering
    • Add fried pakoras to the kadhi and let them soak for a few minutes.
    • Prepare tempering: Heat 1 tbsp oil/ghee, add hing and red chili powder.
    • Pour this over the kadhi for extra flavor.
  5. Garnishing & Serving
    • Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
    • Serve hot with steamed rice or roti.
